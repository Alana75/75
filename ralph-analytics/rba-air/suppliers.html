Contract AI Responsibility Clauses
Mandatory clauses for all supplier contracts — AIR-9.2 · EU AI Act Art.25 · OECD Due Diligence Guidance
Clause 1 — AI System Disclosure
The Supplier shall disclose to Ralph Analytics all AI systems deployed in connection with this engagement, including system purpose, risk tier classification, and governance controls in place, within 30 days of contract execution and annually thereafter by submission of a completed Supplier AI Declaration.
Clause 2 — Minimum AI Governance Standards
The Supplier shall maintain AI governance standards no less stringent than those required by the RBA Code of Conduct Pillar E (AIR), including: a formal AI Ethics Policy approved at executive level; risk classification of all AI systems per the EU AI Act framework; bias testing for AI systems affecting individuals; and human oversight mechanisms for all High Risk AI systems.
Clause 3 — Sub-Tier Flow-Down
The Supplier shall require equivalent AI responsibility standards to be met by its own sub-tier suppliers and contractors deploying AI systems in connection with this engagement, and shall maintain records of sub-tier AI declarations available for review upon request by Ralph Analytics.
Clause 4 — Incident Notification
The Supplier shall notify Ralph Analytics within 30 calendar days of any material AI system change, confirmed AI bias or discriminatory outcome incident, regulatory finding or enforcement action, or deployment of a new AI system that would materially affect this engagement.
Clause 5 — Audit Rights
Ralph Analytics reserves the right to conduct or commission an audit of the Supplier's AI governance practices, including review of AI inventory registers, risk assessments, bias test results, and training records, with reasonable notice not less than 30 days, no more than once per calendar year unless a material incident has occurred.
